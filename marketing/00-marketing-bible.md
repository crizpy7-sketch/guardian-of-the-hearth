# The Shia Baby Marketing Bible (Phase 1 — The Marketing Machine)

> Brand: **Shia & Co.** · Business: Shia Baby · Location: McAllen, TX (serving the Rio Grande Valley)
> Audience: Moms 24–40, expecting mothers, new parents, grandparents, baby-shower shoppers.

---

## 0. Brutal truth before tactics

Read this twice. The rest of the plan only works if you accept it.

1. **You are not a store. You are a feeling.** Nobody drives across McAllen to buy a
   $14 onesie they can get at Target or on Amazon in two days. They come for the
   *experience* and the *emotion* — the wrapped keepsake, the gender-reveal moment, the
   personalized song. Lead with the feeling; the product follows.
2. **The RGV is ~92% Hispanic. If your marketing is English-only, you are invisible to
   most of your market and most of your grandmothers.** Bilingual is not a "nice to
   have." It is the entire game. Many of the highest-spending gift-buyers (abuelas,
   tías) prefer Spanish.
3. **Babies are a referral business.** Pregnant women cluster — same prenatal clinics,
   same churches, same group chats, same workplaces. One delighted mom is worth ten ads.
   Engineer referrals on purpose.
4. **You will live or die on short-form video.** A boutique with no daily TikTok/Reels
   presence in 2026 is a boutique nobody under 40 knows exists.
5. **If you don't capture the phone number, you rented the customer instead of owning
   her.** SMS + email are the only channels you control. Build the list from day one.

## 1. Positioning & Brand Promise

**Positioning statement:**
> For RGV mothers and the families who love them, Shia & Co. is the boutique that turns
> a baby's first moments into keepsakes worth crying over — beautiful, timeless, and
> made personal, in a way Amazon and the big-box stores never can.

**Brand promise (the one line on every wall, bag, and bio):**
> *"Where first moments become forever."* (ES: *"Donde los primeros momentos se vuelven para siempre."*)

**Three pillars (everything we make/post/sell ladders to one):**
1. **Timeless beauty** — cream, powder blue, teddy bears, storybook, heirloom.
2. **Made personal** — songs, names, reveals, keepsakes. The thing only *we* do.
3. **Local & warm** — a McAllen family business that knows your family.

**Brand voice:** Warm, tender, a little poetic, never corporate. Bilingual by default.
Speaks like a loving older sister, not a salesperson.

## 2. The Money Math (so budgets make sense)

You must know these four numbers cold. Estimate now, measure later:

- **AOV** (average order value): assume ~$45 to start. Goal: push to $75+ with bundles.
- **Gross margin:** boutique apparel ~50–60%; personalized/keepsake products ~70–85%
  (this is where the real money is — see §4).
- **CAC** (cost to acquire a customer) target: keep paid CAC under ~$15 early; under
  ~⅓ of first-order gross profit.
- **Repeat rate:** babies grow out of clothes every ~3 months — this category has
  *natural* repeat built in. Capture it or competitors will.

> Rule of thumb for a new single location: spend **$800–$1,500/month** on paid + content
> in the ramp, weighted toward whatever channel is returning. Don't spread it thin across
> all of them — concentrate.

## 3. The Marketing Machine — Channel by Channel

Each channel below has: **role**, **daily actions**, **weekly actions**, **content
ideas**, **hooks/captions**, **viral examples**, **ad ideas**, **budget**, **expected
results**. Channels are ranked by priority for *this* business.

> Universal rule: **shoot once, cut everywhere.** One in-store filming session should
> feed TikTok, Reels, Pinterest Idea Pins, and Facebook for the whole week.

---

### 3.1 TikTok — Priority #1 (discovery engine)

**Role:** Reach strangers. TikTok's algorithm shows good content to people who don't
follow you yet — that's how a McAllen boutique gets seen by a pregnant woman in Edinburg.

**Daily actions**
- Post **1–2 videos/day**, 7–30 seconds. Consistency beats polish.
- Reply to every comment within a few hours (replies feed reach).
- Spend 10 min "studying the feed": save 3 trending sounds/formats to copy tomorrow.

**Weekly actions**
- 1 "emotional anchor" video (a real moment — a reveal, a gift reaction, a song).
- 1 "behind the boutique" video (wrapping, unboxing new stock, owner story).
- 1 trend-jack (use a trending sound over your products).
- Review analytics: double down on the format with the best 3-second retention.

**Content pillars & ideas**
- **The reveal moment:** film (with permission) a gender-reveal box opening in store.
- **The grandma cry:** a grandmother opening a personalized keepsake. (Pure gold.)
- **The song:** play a snippet of a personalized baby song over the baby's photo.
- **ASMR wrapping:** tissue, ribbon, teddy bear, the Shia & Co. box. Oddly satisfying.
- **"Newborn essentials checklist"** — what a first-time mom *actually* needs.
- **Bilingual versions** of your best performers (re-shoot the winner in Spanish).
- **"POV: your baby shower gift is the one everyone talks about."**

**Hooks (first 1–2 seconds — this is 80% of the video)**
- "If you're having a baby in the Valley, save this."
- "This made the grandma cry 🥹" / "Hizo llorar a la abuela 🥹"
- "POV: you found the gift no one else thought of."
- "Things nobody tells first-time moms about newborn clothes…"
- "We turned her name into a song. Watch her face."
- "Don't buy a baby shower gift until you see this."

**Caption template:** Hook line + tiny story + soft CTA + local hashtags.
> "We made baby Mateo his own song 🎶 His mom cried before the chorus. Personalized baby
> songs now at Shia & Co. in McAllen 🤍 #rgv #mcallentx #956 #babyshowergift #shiababy"

**Viral-style examples to model (formats, not exact copies)**
- Boutique owner whispering the story of one product while hands wrap it (low-fi, high-emotion).
- "Rating baby shower gifts from worst to best" ending on your keepsake.
- A silent before/after: empty gift box → finished Shia & Co. presentation.
- "Replying to a comment" videos answering "where do you get cute baby gifts in McAllen?"

**Ad ideas (TikTok Spark Ads = boost your best organic posts)**
- Boost any organic video that hits high saves/shares — don't make ads from scratch first.
- Geo-target a 30–40 mi radius around McAllen, women 24–45 + "engaged shoppers/parents."
- Objective: start with traffic/views to find creative winners, then switch to
  conversions/leads once the website/booking exists.

**Budget:** $0 to start (organic). Add **$150–$400/mo** in Spark Ads boosting proven winners.

**Expected results:** With daily posting, expect a slow 4–8 week ramp, then one or two
videos breaking out (10k–100k+ views) per month once you find your format. Treat the
first 30 videos as paid research, not failures.

### 3.2 Instagram — Priority #2 (trust + conversion)

**Role:** Where people decide whether you're *real* and worth buying from. TikTok gets
them curious; Instagram closes them. The "shop window."

**Daily actions**
- 1 Reel/day (recycle TikToks — remove the TikTok watermark; use CapCut export).
- 3–5 Stories: behind-the-scenes, polls, new arrivals, customer reposts, countdowns.
- Reply to all DMs fast — DMs are where sales actually happen for boutiques.

**Weekly actions**
- 1 polished feed carousel (product styling, "essentials kit," gift guide).
- Update Stories Highlights: *Shop, Keepsakes, Songs, Reveals, Reviews, Visit Us.*
- Repost every customer tag/story (social proof + reciprocity).

**Content ideas**
- Carousels: "5 newborn essentials every Valley mom needs," "Baby shower gifts under $50."
- Reels: same emotional anchors as TikTok.
- Stories: "This or that?" outfit polls; "DM us 'BOX' for a custom keepsake."
- Shoppable posts via Instagram Shopping once your catalog is online.

**Hooks/captions**
- "Save this for your next baby shower 🤍"
- "The gift she'll keep in the memory box forever."
- "Made in the Valley, loved for a lifetime."

**Ad ideas**
- Meta Advantage+ / boosted Reels, same geo + interest targeting as Facebook (§3.3).
- Retarget website + IG engagers with a "complete your gift" offer.

**Budget:** Shares the Meta budget with Facebook (see §3.3): **$300–$600/mo** combined.

**Expected results:** Follower-to-customer trust engine. Watch *saves, shares, DMs,
profile-link taps* — not vanity likes.

### 3.3 Facebook — Priority #3 (where the buyers & grandparents are)

**Role:** Older skew = grandparents and gift-buyers (high AOV). Plus Marketplace, local
Groups, and Events. In the RGV, Facebook is still enormous.

**Daily actions**
- 1 post (can mirror IG). Respond to comments/messages.
- Engage authentically in 2–3 local Facebook Groups (see Local, §3.6).

**Weekly actions**
- 1 Facebook Event for anything happening in-store (trunk show, reveal day, sale).
- 1 customer testimonial/photo post.
- Boost the week's best post to local parents/grandparents.

**Content ideas**
- "Grandparents: the gift that makes you the favorite. 🧸"
- Local pride posts ("Proudly McAllen / Orgullosamente de McAllen").
- Live video walkthroughs of new arrivals (Facebook Live still reaches well).

**Ad ideas (Meta Ads Manager — run IG + FB together)**
- **Engagement → traffic → conversions** ladder as your funnel matures.
- Audiences: 25–55, McAllen + 40mi, interests: baby registry, BabyList, expecting parents,
  baby shower, specific competitor pages.
- **Lookalikes** off your customer list + page engagers (build the list first).
- Retargeting: anyone who watched 50%+ of a video, visited site, or engaged.

**Budget:** **$300–$600/mo** combined Meta. Start broad-ish, let the algorithm find buyers,
then cut losers weekly.

**Expected results:** Your most reliable *paid* revenue channel for gift purchases and
grandparents once pixel + list are seasoned (~30–60 days of data).

### 3.4 Pinterest — Priority #4 (long-tail discovery, free traffic for years)

**Role:** Pinterest is a search engine. Pins drive traffic for *months*. Brides and
expecting moms plan showers/nurseries here. Low effort, compounding return.

**Daily/weekly actions**
- Pin 3–5 vertical images daily (batch weekly). Reuse product + styled photos.
- Create boards: *Nursery Inspo, Baby Shower Gifts, Newborn Essentials, Gender Reveal
  Ideas, Rainbow Baby, Keepsake Gifts, Baby Boy/Girl Outfits.*
- Keyword-stuff (naturally) titles + descriptions: "luxury baby shower gift," "neutral
  nursery," "personalized newborn keepsake."

**Content ideas / pins**
- Styled flat-lays of gift sets. "Newborn essentials checklist" infographic pin.
- Idea Pins (video) recycled from Reels.
- Every blog post / product page → a pin linking back to site.

**Ad ideas:** Optional later. Pinterest's value here is *organic*. Skip ads until you have
budget surplus.

**Budget:** **$0** (time only). Maybe **$50–$100/mo** in promoted pins seasonally (Q4, baby-shower season).

**Expected results:** Slow build, then steady free website traffic that compounds. A
sleeper channel most boutiques ignore — easy edge.

### 3.5 Google — Priority #5 (capture existing intent)

**Role:** When someone searches "baby boutique near me" or "baby shower gifts McAllen,"
you must be there. This is **demand capture**, the highest-intent traffic you can get.

**Foundational (do this week, free):**
- **Google Business Profile** fully optimized: photos, hours, categories ("Baby store,"
  "Gift shop"), products, posts, Q&A. This is the single highest-ROI free thing you can do.
- Get reviews relentlessly (see referral/§3.8). Aim for 50+ reviews, 4.8★+.
- Local SEO: name-address-phone consistent everywhere (Yelp, Apple Maps, FB).

**Weekly actions**
- Post a Google Business "update" weekly (offers, new arrivals, events).
- Respond to every review (yes, even the good ones).

**Ad ideas**
- **Google Search Ads** on high-intent local terms: "baby boutique mcallen," "baby
  shower gift mcallen," "gender reveal mcallen," "personalized baby gift." Small budget,
  tight geo, exact/phrase match. These convert because intent is already there.
- **Performance Max** later, once you have a real product feed.

**Budget:** GBP = free. Search Ads **$150–$300/mo** on a tight local keyword set.

**Expected results:** Low volume, high conversion. The "ready to buy right now" customer.
GBP optimization alone can noticeably lift walk-ins within weeks.

### 3.6 Local & Community — Priority (co-#1 with TikTok for a local boutique)

**Role:** You are a *local* business. This is your unfair advantage over Amazon. Own the
RGV community and you own a moat no algorithm can take.

**Daily/weekly actions**
- Be active (helpfully, not spammy) in RGV Facebook Groups: McAllen moms, RGV moms,
  "buy/sell/trade," parenting groups. Answer questions; mention store only when relevant.
- Build relationships with **adjacent local businesses**: OB/GYN offices, birth
  photographers, doulas, maternity boutiques, cake/dessert shops (reveals!), event
  planners, daycares, churches.

**High-leverage local plays**
- **Cross-referral partnerships:** birth photographer hands your card; you display her
  work. Same with reveal cake bakers, party rental companies.
- **Hospital/clinic gift relationships:** a small Shia & Co. gift for new moms at a local
  birthing center = brand impressions at the most emotional moment of their lives.
- **Local events/markets:** pop-up booths at RGV markets, baby expos, quinceañera/bridal
  expos (future moms), church bazaars. A beautiful booth = walking billboard + email list.
- **Sponsor** a local moms' group meetup or a small charity (NICU support, foster/rainbow
  baby causes — emotionally aligned with your brand).

**Budget:** **$100–$300/mo** for booth fees, samples, partnership gifts.

**Expected results:** The most durable, highest-trust growth. Slow to build, nearly
impossible for competitors to copy. This is your moat.

### 3.7 In-Store Marketing — Priority (turns visits into shares & repeat)

**Role:** The store itself is media. Every visitor should leave with (a) a photo they
posted and (b) a reason to come back.

**Tactics**
- **A signature photo moment:** a teddy-bear/storybook wall, a "Shia & Co." arch, a
  bassinet vignette — beautiful enough that customers *want* to photograph and tag it.
  Put your @handle + a tiny sign: "Tag us @shiababy 🤍 for a surprise."
- **Premium unboxing in-store:** cream box, tissue, ribbon, a tiny handwritten note. The
  packaging *is* the marketing (it gets filmed and gifted).
- **Capture at the register:** "Want 10% off today + first to know about new arrivals?
  Just your phone & email." (List building — non-negotiable.)
- **Keepsake/song display:** a station where customers hear a sample personalized song
  and see keepsake examples. Demo the thing competitors can't sell.
- **Loyalty card / "Baby Milestone Club"** (see §3.8).
- **Seasonal window/displays** that double as photo backdrops and TikTok sets.

**Budget:** **$200–$500 one-time** for the photo moment + signage; **$50–$100/mo** for
packaging materials and refreshes.

**Expected results:** Higher AOV, more user-generated content, more list signups, more
repeat visits. The store earns its rent twice — once in sales, once in content.

### 3.8 Customer Referral & Loyalty System — Priority (cheapest growth there is)

**Role:** Turn one mom into three. Babies travel in social packs; weaponize word of mouth.

**The "Bring a Mama" referral program**
- Give every customer a referral code/card: *"Give a friend $10, get $10."* (Or a free
  keepsake item for the referrer after a friend's first purchase.)
- Pregnant women share genuinely good finds — make sharing effortless and rewarded.

**The "Baby Milestone Club" (loyalty + repeat engine)**
- Sign up at register with **due date + baby's birthday**.
- Automated SMS/email at milestones: newborn → 3mo → 6mo → 1st birthday. Each pings them
  *exactly* when they need the next clothing size or a milestone keepsake.
- Birthday reward: a free small gift or discount on baby's birthday month. (Drives repeat
  + emotional loyalty.)
- Points or punch-card: "5th outfit, keepsake on us."

**Review engine**
- After every purchase: SMS "Loved your visit? A quick Google review means the world to a
  small family business 🤍 [link]." Offer a tiny incentive (sticker, raffle entry — stay
  within platform rules; don't pay for reviews directly).

**Budget:** Cost of incentives only (~5–10% of referred-order value). Effectively negative
CAC.

**Expected results:** Over time, referrals + repeat should become 30–50% of revenue. This
is the difference between a boutique that survives and one that thrives.

### 3.9 Email & SMS Marketing — Priority (the channel you OWN)

**Role:** Algorithms can cut your reach overnight; your list can't be taken away. SMS
gets ~98% open rates. This is where repeat revenue lives.

**Setup**
- Tool: Klaviyo (or Mailchimp + a simple SMS app) — pick one and start. Collect email +
  phone at register, on site, and via "DM us" / "text us" CTAs.
- Always get explicit opt-in for SMS (legal requirement). Keep texts rare and valuable.

**Automated flows (set once, earn forever)**
1. **Welcome flow:** "Welcome to the Shia & Co. family 🤍 here's 10% off your first visit."
2. **Abandoned cart/browse** (once site exists).
3. **Milestone flow** (the Baby Milestone Club, §3.8) — the crown jewel.
4. **Post-purchase:** thank-you + review request + cross-sell a keepsake.
5. **Win-back:** "We miss you — baby's grown! Here's what's new for [size]."

**Campaign cadence**
- Email: 1–2/week (new arrivals, gift guides, story-driven).
- SMS: 2–4/month max (new drops, events, time-sensitive offers). Over-texting = unsubscribes.

**Content ideas/captions**
- "New arrivals just landed 🤍 the cream collection you've been waiting for."
- "Baby shower this month? We'll wrap it beautifully — reply YES and we'll help you pick."
- Spanish versions for the segment that prefers it (segment by language preference!).

**Budget:** **$0–$60/mo** for tooling at small list sizes. Scales with list.

**Expected results:** Email/SMS commonly drives **20–30%+ of e-commerce revenue** for
boutiques. Your highest-ROI channel once the list is built. **Build the list from day one.**

### 3.10 Influencer / Creator Strategy — Priority (borrow trust)

**Role:** RGV mom-influencers and creators already have the exact audience you want.
Micro/nano creators (2k–50k local followers) convert better and cost less than big names.

**Daily/weekly actions**
- Build a list of 20–30 RGV mom creators, birth photographers, and local lifestyle
  accounts. DM 3–5/week.
- Gift product (a beautiful keepsake or gift set) in exchange for an honest post/Reel.

**Program structure**
- **Tier 1 — Gifting (nano/micro):** free product for a tagged Story/Reel. Low cost, high
  authenticity. Run continuously.
- **Tier 2 — Paid collabs (mid):** $50–$300 per Reel from creators who drove real traffic.
- **Tier 3 — Ambassadors:** 3–5 favorite local moms get ongoing product + a referral code;
  they become the face of Shia & Co. in their networks.
- **UGC creators:** pay a creator to make TikToks *for your account* (you don't even need
  their audience — you need the content volume).

**Content asks (be specific):**
- "Unbox the gift set and react." / "Show the keepsake to your mom on camera." / "Play the
  baby song for the first time on video."

**Budget:** **$100–$400/mo** (mostly product cost early; paid collabs as you find winners).

**Expected results:** Trust + content volume + new audiences. Track each creator with a
unique code/link; keep the ones who actually sell.

### 3.11 Grand Opening Campaign — the launch detonator

**Role:** Manufacture a moment big enough to put Shia & Co. on the RGV map in one weekend.

**Pre-launch (4 weeks out)**
- "Coming soon" teaser everywhere. Countdown Stories. Build an early SMS/email list with a
  "VIP first-look" signup.
- Seed 5–10 local creators with product + invites *before* opening.
- Run a giveaway: "Win a $250 Shia & Co. newborn dream bundle — follow, tag 2 expecting/new
  moms, share to your story." (Explodes reach + builds list.)

**Launch weekend**
- **Ribbon-cutting + party:** balloons, the photo wall, free wrapping, a "first 50 guests
  get a free keepsake," live personalized-song demos.
- Invite local press (The Monitor, local TV morning shows, RGV lifestyle pages) and the
  McAllen Chamber of Commerce. A ribbon-cutting is free press.
- Live-stream parts of it on FB/IG. Capture *tons* of content for the next month.
- Collect every attendee's phone/email (raffle entry = list building).

**Launch offers**
- Grand-opening gift with purchase; a limited "founding family" perk (early Milestone Club
  members get a permanent small perk — creates loyalty + urgency).

**Budget:** **$1,000–$3,000 one-time** (decor, giveaways, refreshments, a small ad push,
press outreach). The content and list it generates pay it back for months.

**Expected results:** A spike in followers, a seeded SMS/email list, a library of content,
and local awareness you'd otherwise spend months building.

## 4. The Profit Truth: lead with what only YOU can sell

Apparel is your *traffic*. **Personalized & keepsake products are your profit and your
moat.** Margins are far higher and Amazon literally cannot compete:

| Product | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Personalized baby songs** | ~Pure-margin, deeply emotional, infinitely shareable on video, unique to you. Your single most differentiated product. *Make it the hero.* |
| **Keepsake gifts** (name keepsakes, milestone sets, footprint/handprint, memory boxes) | High margin, high emotion, gift-able, photographs beautifully. |
| **Gender-reveal experiences** | High ticket, high drama, generates the best video content you'll ever get. |
| **Rainbow baby gifts** | Profound emotional resonance; an underserved, loyal niche. |
| **Curated gift bundles** | Raise AOV from ~$45 to $75–$150; reduce decision fatigue. |

**Strategy:** Use cute apparel/ads to pull people in, then guide them to the
high-margin, high-emotion personalized line. Every apparel sale should offer a keepsake
add-on at the register and in post-purchase SMS.

## 5. Weekly Operating Rhythm (so this actually gets done)

**Solo-owner-realistic** plan. ~1 batch shoot + daily posting.

- **Sunday (90 min):** Batch-film 7–14 short videos. Plan the week's posts. Schedule
  email/SMS.
- **Daily (20–30 min):** Post to TikTok + IG Reel + Stories. Reply to all comments/DMs.
  Pin 3–5 Pinterest pins. Post 1 Google Business update (or weekly).
- **Mid-week (30 min):** Engage in 2–3 local FB groups. DM 3–5 creators. Check ad spend,
  cut losers.
- **Friday (30 min):** Review analytics — what got saved/shared/DM'd? Double down next week.
- **Monthly:** Review the four numbers (§2), refresh in-store displays, plan next month's
  calendar and budget.

## 7. What "good" looks like (early KPIs)

- TikTok/Reels: rising saves & shares; ≥1 breakout video/month by month 2–3.
- Email/SMS list: growing every single week (this is the leading indicator of future revenue).
- Google Business: 50+ reviews at 4.8★+ within ~6 months.
- Repeat + referral: trending toward 30%+ of revenue by month 6–12.
- AOV: climbing from ~$45 toward $75+ via bundles and keepsake add-ons.

> Track *leading* indicators (list growth, saves, DMs, reviews) weekly. Track *lagging*
> indicators (revenue, AOV, repeat rate) monthly. Adjust the dials accordingly.
